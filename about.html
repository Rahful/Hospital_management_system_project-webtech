Dhaka Medical College Hospital(1946-PRESENT)

Dhaka Medical College and Hospital was established in 1946 during British colonial rule. Since its establishment, Dhaka Medical College is continuously playing a pioneering role in dispersing medical education among young pupils. The hospital attached with the college provides affordable health care to a huge number of patients through its outdoor, indoor and emergency facilities. Over time, the original 100-bed hospital has grown to become Bangladesh's largest hospital. After adding the 500 beds in 2013, it now contains 2300 beds.

Presently the hospital is functioning full swing with only 40% of approved manpower including indoor, outdoor, surgery, pathology, radiology, dialysis and burn unit etc. Due to paucity of manpower, budget and other logistics, some departments, like Blood bank, ICU, CCU, Neuro-medicine, Nero-Surgery, Psychiatry, physical medicine etc. are yet to begin.

Hospital is now vibrant with almost 21-2500 outdoor patients and 300-350 indoor patients daily.Since its journey began, it has proved its uniqueness by serving humanity and working towards only proposed green hospital in public sector.
